Common Questions

Things people ask before booking

What happens in the 45-minute call?
It's a real conversation, not a survey. We'll talk through a typical week, after-school pickups, the invisible weight you carry, what you've already tried, and what you'd do with more breathing room. It feels like talking to a knowledgeable friend.
Should both of us be on the call?
Just the parent who manages most of the day-to-day. The report is written so your partner can read it too and finally understand what you've been carrying, sometimes for the first time.
What does the report actually look like?
It's a document written specifically about your family. Your names, your schedules, your actual situation, in the language that came out of your own conversation. It is not a template with your name at the top.
Is this connected to the dashboard?
The Brief is its own thing and worth it on its own. The Family Dashboard (coming soon) is a subscription product built from your Brief findings, so the conversation becomes the setup. But there's no pressure to go further. The $75 stands alone.
